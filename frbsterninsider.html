The Stern Insider

July 2015
The Stern Insider is a monthly update on alumni news, career opportunities, timely faculty research and expert opinion, campus news, recent events and more.
 

Alumni in the News

Your next investment: the franchise. Allan Boomer, MBA '04, discusses his journey into the world of fitness franchises and the steps he took before investing.

Woman on the rise. Carine Jean-Claude, BS '89, Chief Compliance Officer at Arrow Electronics, Inc., is named a "Woman Worth Watching" by the Profiles in Diversity Journal.

Wine and the millennial. Adam Teeter, MBA '14, and business partner launch VinePair, an online wine magazine for millennials.

Data that drives employee satisfaction. John Callery, MSBA '14, Global Head of Workforce Strategies at BNY Mellon, discusses workplace analytics as a means of recruiting and keeping top talent.

Faculty News & Research

Hands off China’s stock market. In a CNBC op-ed, Professors Jennifer Carpenter and Robert Whitelaw say China should be playing a long-term game.

Is Uber the Answer to NYC’s Traffic Jam? Professor Arun Sundararajan explains why TLC supply limits won't work, but real ridesharing efforts might in The New York Times.

What's next for Greece? Professor Nicholas Economides talks with Bloomberg about the need for political reform and economic growth as Greece negotiates its bailout deal.

Professor Lasse Pedersen explains the value of an Efficiently Inefficient market for active investors. Hear more about his new book via CNBC.

Did You Know...

Which are the most overvalued and undervalued social media companies today? Watch Professors Scott Galloway and Aswath Damodaran discuss this hot topic.

Stern on the Scene

Stern Goes West: This summer, Twitter’s Mike Gupta, BS '92, joined Prof. Al Lieberman and Bay Area alumni for a conversation on technology’s changing landscape. In Los Angeles, an all-alumni panel discussed trends in marketing and brand management.

Business in the Age of Big Data: More than 100 faculty, alumni and practitioners gathered this June for the 1st Annual Business Analytics Symposium, themed "Transforming the Workplace."
